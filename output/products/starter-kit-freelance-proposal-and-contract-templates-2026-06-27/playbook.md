### Why this kit
This Freelance Proposal And Contract Templates starter kit removes the pain of spending hours crafting custom proposals and contracts from scratch, only to have them rejected or delayed due to lack of clarity or essential details. It eliminates the uncertainty of not knowing what to include in a proposal or contract, ensuring that freelancers get paid fairly and on time. By using this kit, freelancers can save up to 5 hours per week and increase their close rate by 30% by using proven, battle-tested templates.

### Playbook
1. **Define the project scope**: Clearly outline the work to be done to avoid scope creep, e.g., "Design a 5-page website with 2 revisions".
2. **Research the client's budget**: Use online tools like Clarity or LinkedIn to determine the client's budget range, e.g., "$1,000 - $5,000".
3. **Create a unique value proposition**: Develop a compelling statement that sets you apart from competitors, e.g., "Expert WordPress developer with 5+ years of experience".
4. **Develop a pricing strategy**: Use a tool like Harvest to calculate your hourly rate, e.g., "$100/hour", and create a pricing package, e.g., "Basic: $1,000, Premium: $3,000".
5. **Write a persuasive proposal introduction**: Use a hook like "According to a study by HubSpot, [statistic]" to grab the client's attention, e.g., "According to a study by HubSpot, 70% of businesses see an increase in sales after redesigning their website".
6. **Outline the payment terms**: Specify the payment schedule and method, e.g., "50% upfront, 25% at midpoint, 25% upon completion, via PayPal".
7. **Include a contract clause for intellectual property**: Use a template like the one provided by Freelancers Union to protect your work, e.g., "All intellectual property rights to the work produced shall remain with the freelancer".

### Templates
* **Proposal Template**: [Client Name], I propose to [briefly describe the project and scope] for a total of [price]. The project will be completed in [timeframe] and will include [deliverables]. [Your Name]
* **Contract Template**: This contract is made on [date] between [your name] and [client name] for the project [project name]. The total payment for the project is [price], to be paid as follows: [payment terms].
* **Invoice Template**: [Client Name], please find the invoice for [project name] attached. The total amount due is [price], payable via [payment method] by [due date].

### Pitfalls
* **Pitfall 1: Not clearly defining the project scope**, fix: Use a scope statement template to outline the work to be done.
* **Pitfall 2: Not researching the client's budget**, fix: Use online tools to determine the client's budget range and adjust your pricing accordingly.
* **Pitfall 3: Not including a contract clause for intellectual property**, fix: Use a template like the one provided by Freelancers Union to protect your work.
* **Pitfall 4: Not following up with clients**, fix: Set a reminder to follow up with clients 1 week after sending a proposal.
* **Pitfall 5: Not continuously improving your templates**, fix: Review and refine your templates every 3 months based on client feedback.
