### Why this kit
This Personal Finance And Budgeting Toolkit removes the pain of living paycheck to paycheck, eliminates the stress of unexpected expenses, and helps you build a safety net of $1,000 in just 3 months. It's designed for individuals who feel overwhelmed by their finances and want a clear, step-by-step plan to achieve financial stability. By using this kit, you'll be able to track your expenses, create a budget, and make informed decisions about your money, freeing up $500 per month for savings and debt repayment.

### Playbook
1. **Track every transaction** for 30 days using the Mint app to identify areas where you can cut back, like the $500 you spent on dining out last month.
2. **Categorize expenses** into needs (housing, food, utilities) and wants (entertainment, hobbies) to allocate 50% of your income towards necessary expenses.
3. **Set a savings goal** of $1,000 in 3 months, which translates to setting aside $333 per month, or $83 per week.
4. **Create a budget** using the 50/30/20 rule, where 50% of your income goes towards necessary expenses, 30% towards discretionary spending, and 20% towards saving and debt repayment.
5. **Prioritize high-interest debt** by focusing on the credit card with the highest APR, such as the one with a 22% interest rate, and paying $200 extra per month towards the principal.
6. **Use the debt snowball method** to pay off smaller debts first, like the $1,000 credit card balance, to build momentum and confidence.
7. **Automate savings** by setting up a monthly transfer of $100 from your checking account to your savings account using the Capital One 360 app.
8. **Review and adjust** your budget every 3 months to ensure you're on track to meet your financial goals, such as increasing your income by 10% or reducing your expenses by 5%.
9. **Consider used or refurbished items** instead of buying new, like purchasing a refurbished laptop for $300 instead of a brand new one for $1,000.
10. **Use cashback apps** like Ibotta and Rakuten to earn an average of 5% cashback on your grocery purchases, which can add up to $100 per month.

### 30-day plan
* Week 1: Track every transaction and categorize expenses, with a goal of reducing dining out expenses by 20%.
* Week 2: Create a budget and set a savings goal, aiming to allocate 10% of your income towards savings.
* Week 3: Prioritize high-interest debt and make a plan to pay off $500 of debt, with a goal of paying $200 extra per month towards the principal.
* Week 4: Automate savings and review progress, with a goal of increasing your income by 5% or reducing your expenses by 5%.

### Pitfalls
* **Not tracking expenses**: Failing to track every transaction can lead to overspending and a lack of awareness about where your money is going. Fix: Use the Mint app to track every transaction for 30 days.
* **Not prioritizing high-interest debt**: Failing to prioritize high-interest debt can lead to paying more in interest over time. Fix: Focus on paying off the credit card with the highest APR first.
* **Not automating savings**: Failing to automate savings can lead to inconsistent saving and a lack of progress towards financial goals. Fix: Set up a monthly transfer of $100 from your checking account to your savings account.
* **Not reviewing and adjusting**: Failing to review and adjust your budget regularly can lead to stagnation and a lack of progress towards financial goals. Fix: Review your budget every 3 months and make adjustments as needed.
* **Not considering used or refurbished items**: Failing to consider used or refurbished items can lead to overspending on new items. Fix: Research used or refurbished options before making a purchase.
* **Not using cashback apps**: Failing to use cashback apps can lead to missing out on potential savings. Fix: Download and use cashback apps like Ibotta and Rakuten for your grocery purchases.
