Playbook

  1. Identify target market segments using tools like Google Trends and Ahrefs to analyze search volume and competition, e.g., "enterprise software" has 2,900 searches per month.
  2. Conduct competitor analysis using SEMrush to assess website traffic and social media engagement, e.g., analyzing Microsoft's website traffic reveals a 25% increase in the last quarter.
  3. Develop a unique value proposition (UVP) using the Value Proposition Canvas, e.g., "our software increases sales productivity by 15% through automated lead generation".
  4. Create a minimum viable product (MVP) roadmap using Trello or Asana to prioritize features, e.g., "develop a functional prototype within 6 weeks with a budget of $10,000".
  5. Establish key performance indicators (KPIs) using Mixpanel or Amplitude to track user engagement, e.g., "increase daily active users by 20% within 3 months".
  6. Build a sales funnel using HubSpot or Salesforce to manage leads, e.g., "generate 50 new leads per month through targeted LinkedIn ads".
  7. Develop a content marketing strategy using WordPress or Medium to create engaging blog posts, e.g., "publish 2 articles per week on industry trends and best practices".
  8. Utilize social media advertising using Facebook Ads or Twitter Ads to reach a wider audience, e.g., "allocate $500 per month for targeted ads on Facebook and Twitter".
  9. Analyze customer feedback using SurveyMonkey or Typeform to improve product development, e.g., "collect feedback from 100 customers within 2 weeks to inform product roadmap".
  10. Monitor industry trends using Crunchbase or CB Insights to stay ahead of the competition, e.g., "track 5 key competitors and analyze their funding and acquisition activity".

Pitfalls

  • Mistake: Not validating assumptions about target market segments, fix: use customer surveys and feedback to validate assumptions.
  • Mistake: Not prioritizing features for MVP development, fix: use the MoSCoW method to prioritize features.
  • Mistake: Not tracking KPIs regularly, fix: set up a dashboard using Mixpanel or Amplitude to track KPIs daily.
  • Mistake: Not adjusting sales funnel strategy based on results, fix: analyze conversion rates and adjust the sales funnel strategy quarterly.
  • Mistake: Not staying up-to-date with industry trends, fix: allocate 1 hour per week to reading industry blogs and news.
  • Mistake: Not using data to inform product development, fix: use customer feedback and usage data to inform product roadmap.
