### Why this kit
This Coffee Taking starter kit removes the pain of awkward networking conversations, where you struggle to build meaningful connections with potential mentors or collaborators. By following this kit, you'll be able to craft a compelling ask and conduct effective coffee meetings, using tools like Calendly to schedule meetings and Trello to track relationships. With this kit, you'll save 5 hours per week typically spent on unproductive networking attempts, and instead, secure at least 2 valuable connections per month.

### Playbook
1. **Define your ask**: Clearly articulate what you want to achieve from the coffee meeting, e.g., "I want to learn about the daily routine of a product manager at Google," to ensure focused conversations.
2. **Research the guest**: Use LinkedIn and Twitter to find common interests and talking points, such as "both of us attended the Web Summit conference," to establish rapport.
3. **Craft a compelling invite**: Use a personalized email template with a specific ask, e.g., "Can we discuss your experience with Agile development over coffee?"
4. **Schedule efficiently**: Utilize Calendly to schedule meetings, reducing back-and-forth emails by 70%, and set reminders 24 hours before the meeting.
5. **Prepare an agenda**: Create a shared Google Doc with 3-5 discussion topics, such as "current projects" and "industry trends," to guide the conversation.
6. **Conduct active listening**: Take notes using Evernote, focusing on key takeaways and action items, such as "follow up on the introduction to the design team."
7. **Follow up**: Send a thank-you email within 24 hours, using a template like "Thanks for the insightful discussion, [Name], I'll [action item]," to solidify the connection.
8. **Track relationships**: Use Trello to monitor the progress of your connections, moving cards from "new connection" to "established relationship" after 3 meetings.
9. **Continuously improve**: Reflect on each meeting using a journaling prompt like "What did I learn, and what would I do differently next time?" to refine your approach.
10. **Expand your network**: Attend at least 2 industry events per quarter, such as Meetup.com gatherings, to meet new potential connections and apply your refined coffee taking skills.

### Templates
* Email invite template: "Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name], and I'm reaching out because [mutual connection]. I'd love to discuss [topic] over coffee. Are you available [proposed dates]?"
* Agenda template: "[Your Name] and [Guest Name] Coffee Meeting Agenda: 1. Introduction, 2. [Topic 1], 3. [Topic 2], 4. Next Steps"
* Follow-up email template: "Thanks for the insightful discussion, [Name]. I'll [action item]. Let's stay in touch and schedule a follow-up meeting in [timeframe]."

### 30-day plan
* Week 1: Define your ask and research 5 potential guests, aiming to send 3 invites by the end of the week, with a goal of scheduling at least 1 meeting.
* Week 2: Craft and send invites to the 5 researched guests, with a goal of securing at least 2 meetings and scheduling them using Calendly.
* Week 3: Prepare agendas and conduct 2 coffee meetings, taking detailed notes and following up with thank-you emails, aiming to establish at least 1 new connection.
* Week 4: Reflect on the meetings, tracking relationships in Trello, and planning the next steps for each connection, with a goal of expanding your network by at least 2 new connections.

### Pitfalls
* **Poor time management**: Fix by scheduling meetings in batches, using Calendly to reduce scheduling time by 50%.
* **Lack of preparation**: Fix by creating a shared agenda and researching the guest, ensuring you have at least 3 discussion topics prepared.
* **Inconsistent follow-up**: Fix by setting reminders and using a follow-up email template, aiming to send thank-you emails within 24 hours of each meeting.
* **Insufficient tracking**: Fix by using Trello to monitor relationships, moving cards from "new connection" to "established relationship" after 3 meetings.
* **Failure to continuously improve**: Fix by reflecting on each meeting, using a journaling prompt to refine your approach, and adjusting your strategy based on the insights gained.

### Next steps
After completing the 30-day plan, focus on scaling your coffee taking efforts by:
* Attending industry events to meet new potential connections
* Expanding your network by introducing connections to each other
* Continuously refining your approach based on feedback and reflection
* Exploring new tools, such as video conferencing software, to expand your reach and increase the efficiency of your coffee taking meetings.
