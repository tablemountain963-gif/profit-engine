Playbook

  1. Research potential clients: Use tools like LinkedIn Sales Navigator and Hunter to find 50 target companies and 100 decision-makers per week, with a focus on companies that have recently raised funding or expanded their teams.
  2. Personalize email subject lines: Increase open rates by 20% by using the recipient's first name and company name in the subject line, as seen in this example: "Meeting invite for [First Name] at [Company Name]".
  3. Use attention-grabbing opening lines: Start your email with a hook that highlights a pain point or a recent achievement of the company, such as "I saw that [Company Name] recently launched a new product, and I think our solution can help increase sales by 15%".
  4. Keep email body concise: Limit your email body to 125 words or less, focusing on the benefits of your solution and including a clear call-to-action, as shown in this example: "Our solution can help [Company Name] reduce costs by 10%. Would you be open to a 15-minute call to discuss further?".
  5. Include a clear call-to-action: Use action-oriented language like "Schedule a call" or "Book a meeting" and include a link to your calendar, such as "Schedule a call with me: [Calendly link]".
  6. Follow up with a phone call: Increase response rates by 25% by following up with a phone call 3 days after sending the initial email, using a script like "Hi [First Name], I wanted to follow up on my email from [Date] and see if you're interested in discussing [Topic]".
  7. Track and optimize email performance: Use tools like Mailchimp and Yesware to track open rates, click-through rates, and response rates, and adjust your email templates and subject lines based on the data, such as increasing the use of personalization if it leads to higher open rates.
  8. Use A/B testing to improve results: Test different subject lines, email bodies, and calls-to-action to determine which ones perform best, such as testing a subject line with the recipient's first name versus one without, and adjust your strategy accordingly.
  9. Segment your email list: Divide your email list into 3 groups based on company size, industry, and job function, and tailor your email templates and subject lines to each group, such as using more technical language for IT professionals.
  10. Continuously update and refine your email list: Remove unresponsive contacts and add new ones, using tools like Datanyze and LeadIQ to find 20 new contacts per week.

Templates

  1. Introduction email: "Hi [First Name], my name is [Your Name] and I'm reaching out from [Your Company]. I saw that [Company Name] is [briefly mention a relevant fact or achievement]. I'd love to discuss how our solution can help [Company Name] [achieve a specific goal]. Schedule a call with me: [Calendly link]"
