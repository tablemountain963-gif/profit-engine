Customer Support Reply Templates Checklist

To provide excellent customer support, you need to respond quickly and effectively to customer inquiries. This checklist provides a comprehensive set of reply templates to help you achieve this goal.

Pre-Response Phase

  • Verify customer's name is spelled correctly in the response using tools like Grammarly or ProWritingAid.
  • Check the customer's order history in Zendesk or Freshdesk to understand their context.
  • Review the customer's previous support interactions using Converse.ai or ManyChat to identify any recurring issues.
  • Set a timer for 2 hours to ensure a timely response, using Toggl or Harvest for tracking.

Response Phase

  • Use a clear and concise subject line, such as "Order #1234: Shipping Update" or "Account Issue: Password Reset".
  • Address the customer's concern directly in the first paragraph, using phrases like "I apologize for the issue" or "I'm happy to help".
  • Provide a specific solution or alternative, such as "We can offer a refund" or "You can try troubleshooting step X".
  • Include a call-to-action, like "Please reply to this email" or "Click this link to resolve the issue".
  • Attach relevant documentation, such as a PDF guide or a JPG screenshot.
  • Why it matters: attaching documentation helps reduce follow-up questions and resolves issues faster.

Post-Response Phase

  • Send a follow-up email after 3 days using Boomerang or FollowUp.cc to check if the issue is resolved.
  • Ask for feedback using a SurveyMonkey or Typeform survey to improve future responses.
  • Update the customer's support ticket in Zendesk or Freshdesk with the response and any additional notes.
  • Review response metrics, such as response time and resolution rate, using Google Analytics or Mixpanel to identify areas for improvement.
  • Why it matters: reviewing metrics helps identify trends and optimize support processes.

Escalation Phase

  • Escalate the issue to a supervisor or specialist if the customer is dissatisfied or the issue is complex, using Slack or Microsoft Teams for communication.
  • Provide a clear explanation of the escalation process, including expected timelines and next steps.
  • Offer a gesture of goodwill, such as a discount or store credit, using CouponCarrier or LoyaltyLion.
