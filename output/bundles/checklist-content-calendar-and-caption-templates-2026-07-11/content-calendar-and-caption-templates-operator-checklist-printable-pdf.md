# Content Calendar Setup
Create a robust content calendar to streamline your social media management, saving you 5 hours per week. By following this checklist, you'll establish a consistent posting schedule using tools like Trello, Google Calendar, or Hootsuite.

### Phase 1: Planning
* Determine your target audience using Facebook Audience Insights or Twitter Analytics
* Identify 5 core topics to focus on, such as industry news or product tutorials
* Set a posting schedule of 3 times per week, using a tool like Buffer to automate
* Allocate 2 hours per week for content creation, using a timer like Tomato Timer
* Research 10 relevant hashtags using Hashtagify or RiteTag
* Develop a unique brand voice, referencing 3-5 key adjectives like "funny" or "professional"

### Phase 2: Content Creation
* Write 10 caption templates using a tool like Canva or Adobe Spark, including:
	+ 2 seasonal templates
	+ 3 promotional templates
	+ 5 educational templates
* Design 5 social media graphics per month, using a consistent color palette
* Source 10 high-quality images per month, using websites like Unsplash or Pexels
* Record 2 videos per quarter, using a camera like a Canon EOS or a smartphone
* Curate 5 user-generated content pieces per month, using a tool like Embedly
* Why it matters: User-generated content increases engagement by 25% 

### Phase 3: Scheduling and Review
* Schedule 4 weeks' worth of content in advance using Hootsuite or Sprout Social
* Review analytics weekly, tracking engagement metrics like likes and comments
* Adjust the content calendar quarterly, based on performance data from Google Analytics
* Monitor brand mentions daily, using a tool like Mention or Brand24
* Share user-generated content weekly, giving credit to the original creator
* Why it matters: Recognizing user-generated content creators increases loyalty by 30%
* Archive 6 months' worth of content, using a cloud storage service like Dropbox
