Playbook

  1. Research your target: Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator to identify 100 potential clients in your niche, with a 50% match rate to your ideal customer profile.
  2. Personalize your subject line: Use the recipient's first name and company name, increasing open rates by 20% according to a study by Yesware.
  3. Keep your email body under 125 words: Use the 3-sentence rule: problem, solution, call-to-action, as recommended by Gary Vaynerchuk.
  4. Use social proof: Mention a mutual connection or a recent achievement, like "Featured in Forbes" or "Partnered with Google".
  5. Optimize your call-to-action: Use a clear and direct ask, like "Schedule a 15-minute call" with a link to your Calendly page.
  6. Follow up with a phone call: Use a tool like PhoneBurner to automate your follow-up calls, increasing response rates by 30%.
  7. Track your metrics: Use a spreadsheet to track your open rates, response rates, and meeting bookings, aiming for a 20% open rate and a 10% response rate.
  8. Refine your targeting: Use data from your first 100 emails to refine your targeting, increasing your match rate to 75%.
  9. Use A/B testing: Test two different subject lines or email bodies to increase your open rate by 15% and your response rate by 10%.
  10. Scale your outreach: Use a tool like Mailshake to automate your email sending, increasing your daily outreach to 50 emails.

Templates

  1. Introduction template: "Hi [First Name], I came across [Company Name] and was impressed by your [Recent Achievement]. I'd love to discuss how [Your Solution] can help you [Specific Pain Point]. [Calendly Link]"
  2. Follow-up template: "Hi [First Name], I wanted to follow up on my previous email about [Specific Pain Point]. Are you available for a quick call this week? [Calendly Link]"
  3. Referral template: "Hi [First Name], I was referred to you by [Mutual Connection]. I'd love to discuss how [Your Solution] can help you [Specific Pain Point]. [Calendly Link]"
  4. Social proof template: "Hi [First Name], I saw that [Company Name] was featured in [Publication]. I'd love to discuss how [Your Solution] can help you [Specific Pain Point]. [Calendly Link]"
  5. Urgency template: "Hi [First Name], I'm offering a limited-time discount on [Your Solution] for the next 10 days. I'd love to discuss how it can help you [Specific Pain Point]. [Calendly Link]"

Pitfalls

  1. Not personalizing your emails: Fix by using the recipient's first name and company name in the subject line and email body.
  2. Using a generic subject line: Fix by using a specific and relevant subject line that mentions the recipient's company or industry.
  3. Not tracking your metrics: Fix by using a spreadsheet to track your open rates, response rates, and meeting bookings.
  4. Not following up: Fix by using a tool like PhoneBurner to automate your follow-up calls.
  5. Not refining your targeting: Fix by using data from your first 100 emails to refine your targeting and increase your match rate.
  6. Not using social proof: Fix by mentioning a mutual connection or a recent achievement in your email body.
