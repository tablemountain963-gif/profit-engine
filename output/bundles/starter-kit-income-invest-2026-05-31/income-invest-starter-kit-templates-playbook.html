Why this kit

This Income Invest starter kit removes the pain of uncertain investment decisions, helping you navigate the complexities of income-generating assets like dividend stocks and real estate investment trusts (REITs). It eliminates the frustration of manually tracking and analyzing investment performance using spreadsheets like Google Sheets. By following this kit, you'll gain clarity on how to allocate $1,000 to $10,000 in income-generating investments.

Playbook

  1. Set a target income goal: Define your desired annual income from investments, e.g., $5,000 from a $50,000 portfolio, using a tool like Personal Capital to track progress.
  2. Choose a brokerage account: Open a tax-advantaged account like a Roth IRA with Fidelity or Vanguard, allowing for $6,000 in annual contributions.
  3. Select dividend stocks: Invest in established dividend payers like Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) or Procter & Gamble (PG), yielding 2.5% to 3.5% annually.
  4. Diversify with REITs: Allocate 20% to 30% of your portfolio to REITs like Realty Income (O) or National Retail Properties (NNN), providing a 4% to 5% annual yield.
  5. Monitor and adjust: Regularly review your portfolio using a tool like Yahoo Finance, rebalancing every 6 months to maintain your target asset allocation.
  6. Tax optimization: Consider tax-loss harvesting with a tool like TurboTax, aiming to reduce your tax liability by $500 to $1,000 annually.
  7. Automate investments: Set up a monthly investment plan with a robo-advisor like Betterment, investing $500 to $1,000 in a diversified portfolio.
  8. Educate yourself: Allocate 1 hour per week to learning about income investing using resources like The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham or the Income Investor podcast.
  9. Review and adjust: Schedule a quarterly review of your portfolio, adjusting your investment strategy as needed to stay on track with your goals.
  10. Dollar-cost average: Invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of market conditions, to reduce timing risks and avoid emotional decision-making.

30-day plan

  • Week 1: Set up a brokerage account and fund it with $1,000 to $5,000, with a goal of earning $20 to $100 in interest or dividends within the first month.
  • Week 2-3: Research and invest in 2-3 dividend stocks, aiming to generate $50 to $200 in dividend income per quarter.
  • Week 4: Allocate 20% to 30% of your portfolio to REITs, targeting a 4% to 5% annual yield and a total income of $100 to $500 per year.

Pitfalls

  • Overdiversification: Avoid investing in too many assets, which can lead to a 10% to 20% decrease in returns due to higher fees and lower yields.
  • Lack of tax planning: Failing to consider tax implications can result in a 10% to 20% reduction in after-tax returns, costing $500 to $2,000 per year.
  • Insufficient research: Investing in assets without proper research can lead to a 20% to 50% loss of principal, resulting in a $1,000 to $5,000 loss.
  • Emotional decision-making: Making investment decisions based on emotions can result in a 10% to 30% decrease in returns, costing $500 to $3,000 per year.
  • Inadequate tracking: Failing to monitor and adjust your portfolio can lead to a 5% to 10% decrease in returns, resulting in a $250 to $1,000 loss per year.
  • Inconsistent investing: Failing to invest regularly can result in a 5% to 10% decrease in returns, costing $250 to $1,000 per year.

Next steps

  • Continue learning: Allocate 1 hour per week to learning about income investing and stay up-to-date with market trends.
  • Review and adjust: Regularly review your portfolio and adjust your investment strategy as needed to stay on track with your goals.
  • Explore advanced strategies: Consider exploring more advanced income investing strategies, such as options trading or real estate crowdfunding, to further diversify your portfolio and increase returns.
