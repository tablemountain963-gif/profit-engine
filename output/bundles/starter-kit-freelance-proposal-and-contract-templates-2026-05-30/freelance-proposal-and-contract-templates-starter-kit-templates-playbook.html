Playbook

  1. Define your niche: Identify your top 3 areas of specialization to increase perceived value and command higher rates, e.g., "I specialize in writing SEO-optimized blog posts for tech startups."
  2. Develop a unique value proposition: Clearly articulate the benefits you bring to clients, such as "I help businesses increase their website traffic by 50% within 3 months."
  3. Create a portfolio: Showcase 5-7 examples of your best work, including case studies and testimonials, using tools like Behance or Contently.
  4. Set clear boundaries: Establish a minimum project size of $1,000 and a maximum workload of 20 hours/week to maintain a healthy work-life balance.
  5. Use a project management tool: Implement Asana or Trello to streamline communication and task assignment with clients.
  6. Develop a contract review process: Schedule a 30-minute call with each new client to review the contract and address any questions or concerns.
  7. Track key metrics: Monitor your proposal close rate, average project value, and client satisfaction rating using a spreadsheet or tool like Google Analytics.
  8. Continuously improve: Schedule a monthly review of your proposal and contract templates to refine your approach and address any issues that arise.

Templates

  • Proposal Template: "Dear [Client], I'm excited to submit this proposal for [Project Name], which includes [Scope of Work] and a total budget of [$X]. Please find the detailed scope and timeline below: [Insert Scope and Timeline]."
  • Contract Template: "This contract is made on [Date] between [Your Name] and [Client Name] for the provision of [Services]. The total fee for the project is [$X], payable in [Number] installments of [$Y] due on [Due Dates]."
  • Invoice Template: "Invoice # [Number] for [Project Name], dated [Date], with a total due of [$X]. Payment is due within 30 days of receipt, with a late fee of 2% per month applied to unpaid balances."

Pitfalls

  • Mistake 1: Failing to clearly define the scope of work, fix: use a detailed scope statement and include a change order process.
  • Mistake 2: Not including a termination clause, fix: add a 30-day notice period for termination and specify the circumstances under which the contract can be terminated.
  • Mistake 3: Not tracking key metrics, fix: use a spreadsheet or tool like Google Analytics to monitor your proposal close rate, average project value, and client satisfaction rating.
  • Mistake 4: Using a generic contract template, fix: customize the contract template to fit your specific business needs and include a contract review process.
  • Mistake 5: Not establishing clear payment terms, fix: specify the payment schedule, late fees, and payment methods in the contract.
