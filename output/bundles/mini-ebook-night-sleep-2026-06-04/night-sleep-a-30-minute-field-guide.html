Ch.2: Setting Your Sleep Schedule

Establishing a consistent sleep schedule is crucial for improving night sleep. This means going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, including weekends. To set your sleep schedule, start by tracking your sleep patterns using a tool like RescueTime or Toggl. This will help you identify your natural sleep-wake cycle and determine the best sleep schedule for your lifestyle.

For example, if you're a morning lark, you may want to set your sleep schedule to wake up at 6:00 AM and go to bed at 10:00 PM. On the other hand, if you're a night owl, you may prefer to wake up at 8:00 AM and go to bed at 12:00 AM. Use a tool like Google Calendar to schedule your sleep and set reminders to ensure you stick to your schedule.

It's also important to consider your circadian rhythm, which is regulated by an internal body clock that responds to light and darkness. Exposure to natural light in the morning helps regulate your circadian rhythm, while exposure to screens and artificial light in the evening can disrupt it. To optimize your circadian rhythm, try to get outside in the morning for at least 10-15 minutes of natural light exposure.

Ch.3: Creating a Sleep-Conducive Environment

Your sleep environment plays a significant role in determining the quality of your night sleep. A sleep-conducive environment is one that is dark, quiet, and cool. To create a sleep-conducive environment, start by investing in blackout curtains or sleep masks to block out any light. The National Sleep Foundation recommends keeping your bedroom at a temperature between 60-67°F (15-19°C) for optimal sleep.
