## Overview
Paced resonance breathing involves breathing in a specific pattern, typically at a rate of around 5-6 breaths per minute, to synchronize the heart rate and breathing. This can lead to a range of benefits, including reduced stress, improved focus, and enhanced overall health. The Breathe CLI project provides a simple and accessible way to practice this technique, using the terminal to guide the user through the breathing pattern.

## Why It Matters
The benefits of paced resonance breathing are backed by scientific research, which has shown that this technique can have a positive impact on the autonomic nervous system. By slowing down the breathing rate and synchronizing it with the heart rate, users can experience a range of benefits, from reduced anxiety and stress to improved sleep quality. With the Breathe CLI project, users can access this technique from the comfort of their own homes, using a tool that is both free and open-source.

## How to Start
To get started with Breathe CLI, users simply need to install the tool on their macOS system and follow the prompts to begin the breathing exercise. The tool will guide the user through the breathing pattern, providing a visual cue to inhale and exhale at the correct times. With regular practice, users can start to experience the benefits of paced resonance breathing for themselves.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall for users of Breathe CLI is the tendency to try to control the breathing too much, rather than allowing the tool to guide them. This can lead to tension and discomfort, rather than the relaxation and calm that the technique is intended to produce. To avoid this, users should focus on allowing themselves to follow the guide, without trying to force their breathing into a specific pattern.
