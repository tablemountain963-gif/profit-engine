## Introduction to Cornell University
25,000 students, 100 academic departments, and a 4,800-acre campus make Cornell University one of the largest and most diverse institutions of higher learning in the United States. With a rich history dating back to 1865, Cornell has established itself as a hub for innovation, research, and conservation efforts. One notable project, led by Cornell University, involves the integration of the Merlin bird ID app with the eBird platform, allowing users to contribute to real-time bird identifications and aid in conservation efforts.

## Overview
Cornell University is composed of 12 colleges and schools, offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The university is known for its academic excellence, with programs in engineering, business, and agriculture being particularly renowned. Cornell is also home to the Lab of Ornithology, a world-leading institution for the study of birds and conservation. The Merlin bird ID app, developed by the Lab, has been a significant contributor to the field of ornithology, allowing users to identify bird species using visual and audio cues.

## Why It Matters
The integration of the Merlin bird ID app with the eBird platform is a significant development in the field of conservation. By allowing users to feed real-time bird identifications into the platform, researchers can gain valuable insights into bird populations, migration patterns, and habitat usage. This information can be used to inform conservation efforts, such as the protection of habitats and the development of effective conservation strategies. With over 100 million bird sightings recorded on the eBird platform, the potential for data-driven conservation is vast.

## How to Start
For individuals interested in contributing to conservation efforts through the Merlin bird ID app and eBird platform, getting started is relatively straightforward. The first step is to download the Merlin bird ID app, which is available for both iOS and Android devices. Once the app is installed, users can begin identifying birds using the app's visual and audio recognition features. The app will then prompt users to submit their sightings to the eBird platform, where the data will be compiled and analyzed by researchers.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall for users of the Merlin bird ID app is the potential for misidentification of bird species. To avoid this, it is essential to use the app's features in conjunction with other identification methods, such as field guides and expertise from experienced birders. Additionally, users should be aware of the importance of data accuracy and take the time to carefully review their submissions before uploading them to the eBird platform.
