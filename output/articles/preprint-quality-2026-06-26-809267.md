### 70% of Preprints Lack Rigorous Review: Boosting Quality with AI
A staggering 70% of preprints on bioRxiv, a popular platform for sharing preliminary research findings, lack rigorous review. This statistic highlights a significant gap in the quality control of preprints, which can have far-reaching implications for the scientific community. To address this issue, researchers have turned to AI assistant tools, such as QED, to score preprint quality. These tools have the potential to revolutionize the way preprints are evaluated, but what does this mean for the future of scientific publishing?

## Overview
Preprints are draft versions of research papers that are shared publicly before undergoing peer review. While they offer a rapid way to disseminate new findings, they often lack the rigorous scrutiny of traditional peer review. This is where AI assistant tools come in – by analyzing preprints and providing a quality score, they can help identify areas for improvement and increase the overall quality of the research. For instance, the QED tool has been used to score preprints on bioRxiv, providing valuable feedback to authors and helping to improve the quality of the research.

## Why It Matters
The quality of preprints is crucial for several reasons. Firstly, preprints are often used to inform policy decisions and guide future research. If the quality of preprints is poor, it can lead to misguided decisions and wasted resources. Secondly, preprints can be used to establish priority of discovery, which can have significant implications for researchers and their careers. Finally, high-quality preprints can help to build trust in the scientific community and promote collaboration among researchers. By using AI assistant tools to evaluate preprint quality, researchers can ensure that their work meets the highest standards of rigor and validity.

## How to Start
So, how can researchers get started with using AI assistant tools to evaluate preprint quality? The first step is to select a tool that aligns with their needs and goals. There are several options available, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. For example, some tools may focus on evaluating the methodology used in the preprint, while others may assess the clarity and coherence of the writing. Once a tool has been selected, researchers can upload their preprint and receive a quality score, along with feedback on areas for improvement.

## Common Pitfalls
While AI assistant tools can be incredibly useful for evaluating preprint quality, there are several common pitfalls to watch out for. One of the most significant challenges is ensuring that the tool is calibrated correctly, so that it provides accurate and reliable feedback. Additionally, researchers must be careful not to rely too heavily on the tool, and instead use it as one part of a broader evaluation process. Finally, it's essential to consider the potential biases and limitations of the tool, and to use multiple tools to get a more comprehensive picture of preprint quality.
