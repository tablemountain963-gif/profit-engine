## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall when selecting tools is the tendency to over-rely on a single tool or platform, rather than considering a more integrated approach. This can lead to **tool fragmentation**, where multiple tools are used in isolation, resulting in inefficiencies and potential compatibility issues. Another pitfall is the **learning curve**, where the time and effort required to learn a new tool can outweigh its potential benefits. By being aware of these pitfalls, professionals can take a more strategic approach to tool selection and adoption.
