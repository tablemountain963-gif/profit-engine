### 85% of Companies Are Unprepared for AI Threat Risk
A staggering 85% of companies have not assessed their software investments for AI threat risk, leaving them vulnerable to potential security breaches. This statistic is alarming, especially considering the increasing reliance on artificial intelligence in various industries. The recent trend of screening all software investments for AI threat risk, as seen in the approach taken by investment firms like Apollo, highlights the growing importance of addressing this issue. 

## Overview
AI threat risk refers to the potential dangers associated with the use of artificial intelligence in software applications. As AI becomes more prevalent, the risk of security breaches, data leaks, and other malicious activities also increases. It is essential for companies to assess their software investments and identify potential AI threat risks to mitigate these dangers. According to recent reports, investment firms are taking proactive steps to screen their software investments for AI threat risk, with some firms reportedly assessing all their investments for potential risks.

## Why It Matters
The stakes are high when it comes to AI threat risk. A single security breach can result in significant financial losses, damage to reputation, and legal liabilities. In fact, the average cost of a data breach is estimated to be around $3.9 million. Furthermore, the use of AI in software applications can also lead to unintended consequences, such as bias in decision-making algorithms or amplification of existing social inequalities. Companies that fail to address AI threat risk may find themselves facing significant challenges in the long run.

## How to Start
To start addressing AI threat risk, companies should take a proactive approach to assessing their software investments. This can involve conducting thorough risk assessments, implementing robust security measures, and ensuring that AI systems are designed with security and transparency in mind. One approach is to use a framework-based methodology, such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, to identify and mitigate potential risks. Additionally, companies can leverage AI-powered security tools to detect and respond to potential threats in real-time.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall is to underestimate the potential risks associated with AI-powered software applications. Companies may assume that AI systems are secure by design, but this is not always the case. Another pitfall is to rely solely on traditional security measures, such as firewalls and antivirus software, which may not be effective against AI-powered threats. Furthermore, companies may also fail to consider the potential risks associated with AI-powered decision-making algorithms, such as bias and amplification of existing social inequalities.

## Recommendations
To address AI threat risk, companies should consider investing in the following product categories:
* **Artificial intelligence security software**, which can help detect and respond to AI-powered threats in real-time
* **Risk assessment and management tools**, which can help companies identify and mitigate potential risks associated with AI-powered software applications
* **AI-powered penetration testing tools**, which can help companies simulate potential attacks and identify vulnerabilities in their AI-powered systems
* **Cybersecurity awareness training platforms**, which can help companies educate their employees on the potential risks associated with AI-powered software applications
* **Incident response and management software**, which can help companies respond quickly and effectively to potential security breaches

By taking a proactive approach to addressing AI threat risk, companies can help mitigate potential dangers and ensure the secure and responsible use of artificial intelligence in their software applications. The next step is to conduct a thorough risk assessment and invest in the necessary tools and technologies to address potential AI threat risks.
