## Introduction to Income
The average American household earns around $67,000 per year, but the labor share of income in the US has hit its lowest post-war level, with workers taking home less than 57% of the national income. This means that for every dollar earned, workers are getting a smaller slice of the pie. Meanwhile, investors are looking for ways to boost their income, with some turning to high-yield exchange-traded funds (ETFs) that offer over 5% passive income. 

## Overview
Income is a critical component of personal finance, and understanding how it works is essential for achieving financial stability. There are various sources of income, including wages, salaries, investments, and entrepreneurship. The labor share of income refers to the proportion of national income that goes to workers, and it has been declining over the years. This decline has significant implications for individuals, businesses, and the overall economy.

## Why It Matters
The decline in labor share of income matters because it affects the purchasing power of workers, which in turn impacts consumer spending and economic growth. When workers earn less, they have less money to spend on goods and services, leading to reduced demand and slower economic growth. Additionally, a lower labor share of income can lead to increased income inequality, as those who own capital and assets tend to benefit more from economic growth. For instance, investors who put their money in high-yield ETFs can earn over 5% passive income, which can be a significant source of revenue.

## How to Start
To start building a stable income stream, individuals can explore various options, such as investing in dividend-paying stocks, real estate investment trusts (REITs), or peer-to-peer lending platforms. They can also consider starting a side business or freelancing to supplement their primary income. Moreover, education and skills training can help individuals increase their earning potential and stay competitive in the job market. For example, learning about personal finance, investing, and entrepreneurship can provide individuals with the knowledge and skills needed to make informed decisions about their money.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall when it comes to income is relying too heavily on a single source of income. This can make individuals vulnerable to financial shocks, such as job loss or market volatility. Another pitfall is failing to diversify investments, which can lead to reduced returns and increased risk. Additionally, not having a clear understanding of personal finance and investing can lead to poor decision-making and financial mistakes. For instance, investing in high-yield ETFs without understanding the underlying assets and risks can result in significant losses.
