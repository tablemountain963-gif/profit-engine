## Introduction to Shine Through

## Overview
Shine-through keycaps are made using a double-shot molding process, where two different colors of plastic are injected into a mold to create the keycap. This process allows for the creation of complex designs and legends that are resistant to wear and tear. The result is a keycap that has a translucent effect, allowing the backlight of the keyboard to shine through the legends. This feature is particularly useful for gamers and typists who work in low-light environments.

## Why It Matters
The shine-through effect is not just a cosmetic feature; it also improves the overall typing experience. With shine-through keycaps, the legends are more visible, even in low-light conditions, which reduces eye strain and improves typing accuracy. Additionally, the double-shot molding process makes the keycaps more durable and resistant to fading, which means they will last longer and maintain their appearance over time. According to some sources, double-shot molded keycaps can last up to 5 times longer than those made using other methods.

## How to Start
To get started with shine-through keycaps, you'll need to determine what type of keyboard you have and what type of switches it uses. Most shine-through keycaps are designed for MX switch keyboards, which are the most common type of switch used in custom keyboards. You'll also need to consider the profile of the keycap, with Cherry profile being one of the most popular. Once you've determined the type of keyboard and switches you have, you can start looking for shine-through keycap sets that are compatible.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall to watch out for when buying shine-through keycaps is the quality of the molding process. Some keycaps may have a uneven or bubbly texture, which can affect the appearance of the legends. Additionally, some keycaps may not be compatible with certain types of switches or keyboards, so it's essential to do your research before making a purchase. Another pitfall is the cost, with high-quality shine-through keycaps often being more expensive than other types of keycaps.
