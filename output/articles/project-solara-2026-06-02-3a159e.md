## Why It Matters
The shift towards devices running AI agents instead of apps is significant because it enables a more personalized and interactive experience. AI agents can learn a user's preferences and adapt to their behavior, making them more efficient and effective. For instance, a smart home device running an AI agent can adjust the lighting and temperature based on the user's schedule and preferences. This technology also has the potential to improve accessibility, as AI agents can assist people with disabilities and make devices more user-friendly.

## Common Pitfalls
One of the common pitfalls of developing devices that run AI agents is the risk of data privacy breaches. As AI agents collect and process large amounts of user data, there is a risk of this data being compromised. To mitigate this risk, developers need to implement robust security measures, such as encryption and access controls. Another pitfall is the potential for AI agents to make biased decisions, which can be addressed by ensuring that the data used to train the agents is diverse and representative.
