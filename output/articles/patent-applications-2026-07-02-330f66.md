85% of patent applications are rejected on the first try, with the average applicant spending over $10,000 in filing fees alone. The high failure rate and significant costs underscore the complexity and importance of navigating the patent application process successfully. 

## Overview
The patent application process is a lengthy and intricate one, involving multiple steps from initial filing to final approval. According to recent rulings, such as the one by Japan's top court, artificial intelligence cannot be listed as an inventor on patent applications, highlighting the evolving landscape of intellectual property law. This decision and others like it affect how inventors and companies approach patent filings, particularly in fields heavily influenced by AI, such as software development and robotics.

## Why It Matters
Patent applications are crucial for protecting intellectual property, encouraging innovation, and providing a competitive edge in the market. The ability to secure patents can significantly impact a company's valuation and future growth prospects. For instance, tech startups often see their valuation increase substantially after securing key patents, as it demonstrates their potential for innovation and dominance in their field. However, the process is not without its challenges, including the high rejection rate mentioned earlier and the lengthy time it takes for applications to be processed, which can be several years.

## How to Start
To begin the patent application process, inventors must first determine the type of patent they need, which could be a utility patent, design patent, or plant patent, depending on their invention. Conducting a thorough patent search is the next step, to ensure the invention is novel and non-obvious. This can involve searching through existing patents and published applications, a process that can be facilitated by patent search software. After determining the invention's eligibility for a patent, the next step is to prepare and file the patent application, which includes a detailed description of the invention, drawings, and claims.

## Common Pitfalls
One of the common pitfalls in the patent application process is inadequate preparation, leading to applications being rejected due to lack of clarity or insufficient detail. Another issue is the failure to conduct a comprehensive patent search, which can result in the invention not being novel or non-obvious, leading to rejection. Additionally, navigating the complexities of patent law, especially with recent rulings on AI inventorship, requires expertise, making it advisable to seek the assistance of a patent attorney.
