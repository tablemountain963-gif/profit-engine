## Overview
The Luttrell Psalter is a rare example of a medieval psalter, a book of psalms used for devotion and prayer. It was likely created for Sir Geoffrey Luttrell, a wealthy landowner in England, and features a mix of biblical scenes, fantastical creatures, and everyday life illustrations. The manuscript's artwork is characterized by its use of gold, blue, and red ink, which were expensive and difficult to produce at the time. With 309 pages of illustrations, the Luttrell Psalter is a treasure trove of medieval art and history.
