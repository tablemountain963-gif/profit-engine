## Introduction to the Playstation Store Controversy
500 movies, purchased by customers through the Playstation Store, are being deleted by Sony. This move has sparked a heated debate about the ownership of digital media and the implications of Digital Rights Management (DRM). The issue at hand is that customers who paid for these movies will no longer have access to them, highlighting the tradeoffs of relying on digital storefronts for media consumption.

## Overview
The Playstation Store is a digital marketplace where customers can purchase and download games, movies, and other forms of digital media. The store offers a wide range of content, including new releases and classic titles. However, the recent decision by Sony to delete over 500 movies has raised concerns about the long-term availability of digital media. This incident is not isolated, as it is a symptom of a broader issue with DRM and the control it gives companies over the media we purchase.

## Why It Matters
The deletion of purchased movies by Sony has significant implications for consumers. It highlights the fact that when we buy digital media, we are not actually owning it, but rather renting it. This is because DRM systems, like those used by the Playstation Store, allow companies to control and restrict access to the media we purchase. This can lead to a situation where customers lose access to their purchased content, often without warning or compensation. According to some critics, this approach undermines the concept of ownership and can be seen as a form of exploitation.

## How to Start
For those looking to build a digital media collection, it is essential to consider the implications of DRM and the potential risks of relying on digital storefronts. One approach is to prioritize purchasing physical copies of media, such as **Blu-ray discs** or **DVDs**, which can provide a sense of ownership and control over the content. Additionally, customers can explore alternative digital marketplaces that offer **DRM-free** options, such as those specializing in **independent films** or **classic movies**.

## Common Pitfalls
When purchasing digital media, it is crucial to be aware of the potential pitfalls. One common issue is the lack of transparency regarding DRM and content availability. Customers may not always be informed about the restrictions on their purchased content, leading to unexpected losses of access. Furthermore, the reliance on digital storefronts can create a sense of complacency, leading customers to overlook the importance of owning physical copies or seeking out DRM-free alternatives.

## Recommendations
To avoid the risks associated with DRM and digital storefronts, customers can consider the following options:
* **Gaming consoles with backwards compatibility**, which allow players to access older games without relying on digital marketplaces
* **External hard drives**, which can provide a secure way to store and back up digital media
* **Media players**, which can offer a flexible and DRM-free way to play back digital content
* **Digital media hubs**, which can provide a centralized platform for managing and accessing digital media collections
By exploring these alternatives, customers can take control of their digital media and avoid the risks associated with relying on digital storefronts.

## Conclusion
The deletion of over 500 movies from the Playstation Store serves as a reminder of the importance of considering the implications of DRM and digital ownership. By prioritizing physical copies, exploring alternative marketplaces, and seeking out DRM-free options, customers can take control of their digital media and avoid the risks associated with relying on digital storefronts. The next step is to start exploring these alternatives and building a digital media collection that is secure, flexible, and truly owned.
