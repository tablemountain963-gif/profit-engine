85% of companies that adopt AI solutions fail to achieve their expected returns, largely due to the high costs and complexities associated with building or buying AI systems. However, a growing number of CEOs are turning to a more cost-effective and flexible approach: DIY AI. This approach allows companies to develop and implement their own AI solutions in-house, using existing resources and personnel. The cost savings can be substantial, with some companies reporting reductions of up to 70% compared to traditional build or buy methods.

## Overview
DIY AI involves using pre-trained models, open-source software, and other readily available tools to develop and deploy AI solutions. This approach requires some technical expertise, but it can be more cost-effective and flexible than building or buying AI systems from scratch. According to recent trends, DIY AI is becoming increasingly popular among businesses, with many CEOs choosing this approach over traditional methods.

## Why It Matters
The traditional build or buy approach to AI can be costly and time-consuming, requiring significant investments in personnel, hardware, and software. In contrast, DIY AI allows companies to leverage existing resources and personnel to develop and deploy AI solutions quickly and efficiently. This approach also enables companies to tailor their AI solutions to their specific needs and requirements, rather than relying on off-the-shelf products. Furthermore, DIY AI can help companies to develop the internal expertise and capabilities they need to succeed in an AI-driven world.

## How to Start
To get started with DIY AI, companies need to assess their existing resources and personnel, and identify areas where they can leverage AI to improve their operations. This may involve conducting a thorough analysis of their business processes and identifying opportunities for automation and optimization. Companies should also consider investing in AI training and education for their personnel, to ensure they have the necessary skills and expertise to develop and deploy AI solutions. Additionally, companies can explore various AI frameworks and tools, such as machine learning libraries and natural language processing tools, to support their DIY AI efforts.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall of DIY AI is the lack of expertise and resources, which can lead to poor-quality AI solutions that fail to deliver expected returns. Another pitfall is the risk of data quality issues, which can compromise the accuracy and effectiveness of AI models. Companies should also be aware of the potential risks associated with open-source software and pre-trained models, such as security vulnerabilities and intellectual property issues. To mitigate these risks, companies should invest in robust testing and validation procedures, and ensure that their AI solutions are thoroughly evaluated and refined before deployment.

## Recommendations
To succeed with DIY AI, companies should consider investing in the following product categories:
* **Cloud-based machine learning platforms**, which provide pre-trained models and scalable infrastructure for developing and deploying AI solutions
* **Natural language processing tools**, which enable companies to analyze and generate human language data
* **Deep learning frameworks**, which provide pre-built functions and libraries for developing and training AI models
* **Data analytics software**, which enable companies to collect, process, and analyze large datasets
* **AI-powered automation tools**, which enable companies to automate repetitive and mundane tasks

By leveraging these product categories and adopting a DIY AI approach, companies can develop and deploy effective AI solutions that drive business value and improve operations.

To get started with DIY AI, companies should take a clear next step: assess their existing resources and personnel, and identify areas where they can leverage AI to improve their operations. By doing so, companies can unlock the full potential of AI and achieve significant cost savings, improved efficiency, and enhanced competitiveness in the market.
