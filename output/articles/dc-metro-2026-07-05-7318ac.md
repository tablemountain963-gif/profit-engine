## Overview
The DC Metro is a 117-mile rapid transit system that serves the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area. It has 91 stations and operates on a complex network of lines, including the Red, Orange, Silver, Blue, Yellow, and Green lines. The system is operated by the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA) and is used by millions of people every day. Despite its importance, the DC Metro faces numerous challenges, including aging infrastructure, funding issues, and concerns about safety and security.
