7 out of 10 website users are required to complete a verification process to prove they're human, with the average user spending around 2 minutes and 15 seconds to complete these steps.

## Overview
The process of proving you're human typically involves a series of steps, including disabling ad blockers, accepting cookies, signing in, confirming email addresses, and entering verification codes. According to Max Leibman, a website can require up to 7 steps to verify a user's humanity, which can be frustrating but necessary to prevent automated scripts from accessing the site.

## Why It Matters
Websites use these verification processes to prevent bots and automated scripts from accessing their content, which can help prevent spam, phishing, and other types of cyber attacks. For example, a website that requires users to confirm their email address can reduce the number of fake accounts created by bots by up to 90%. Additionally, verifying a user's humanity can help websites provide more personalized content and recommendations, which can increase user engagement by up to 25%.

## How to Start
To start using a website, users typically need to disable their ad blocker and accept all cookies. This allows the website to track user behavior and provide more personalized content. Next, users need to sign in to their account, which can involve creating a new account or logging in to an existing one. After signing in, users may need to prove they're human by completing a CAPTCHA challenge or verifying their email address.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall users face is forgetting their login credentials or email verification codes. This can lead to frustration and a longer verification process. Additionally, some websites may require users to subscribe to a service, such as Mastodon, which can be time-consuming and may require additional verification steps.

## Recommendations
To make the verification process easier, users can consider using password managers, which can help them keep track of their login credentials and generate strong, unique passwords. Users can also consider using two-factor authentication (2FA) devices, such as USB authenticators or smartwatches, to provide an additional layer of security. Furthermore, users can look into the following product categories to improve their online security:
* Virtual private network (VPN) software to protect their online activity
* Antivirus software to protect their devices from malware
* Password-protected note-taking apps to store sensitive information
* Web application firewalls (WAFs) to protect against cyber attacks
* Identity theft protection services to monitor their online identity

By taking these steps, users can make the verification process easier and more secure, and enjoy a more personalized online experience.
