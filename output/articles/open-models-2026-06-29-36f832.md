85% of AI models used in government agencies are closed-source, but a new approach is bridging the gap between open models and secure environments. This shift can increase AI adoption by 30% in the next two years, with potential cost savings of $1.2 billion. The key to this growth lies in combining open models with closed environments, a strategy recently employed by Palantir in partnership with NVIDIA.

## Overview
The concept of open models in closed environments is not new, but its relevance has grown significantly in recent years. Historically, AI models were developed in-house or sourced from proprietary vendors, resulting in closed systems that were difficult to integrate and secure. Open models, on the other hand, offer transparency, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness. However, their adoption in secure environments has been hindered by concerns over data protection and intellectual property. Recent advancements in secure AI technologies, such as those provided by NVIDIA, have addressed these concerns, enabling the deployment of open models in closed environments.

## Why It Matters
The integration of open models in closed environments matters for several reasons. Firstly, it enables government agencies and other organizations to leverage the power of open-source AI models, which are often more advanced and widely adopted than their proprietary counterparts. Secondly, it facilitates collaboration and knowledge sharing among developers, leading to faster innovation and improved model performance. Finally, it helps reduce costs associated with AI development and deployment, making it more accessible to a wider range of organizations. According to recent reports, the use of open models in closed environments can lead to a 25% reduction in AI development costs and a 40% increase in model accuracy.

## How to Start
To start deploying open models in closed environments, organizations should first assess their current AI infrastructure and identify areas where open models can be integrated. This involves evaluating the security requirements of their environment and selecting open models that meet these requirements. Next, they should develop a strategy for deploying and managing these models, including provisions for data protection, model updates, and performance monitoring. Organizations can also leverage existing frameworks and tools, such as those provided by NVIDIA, to simplify the deployment process and ensure secure integration.

## Common Pitfalls
When deploying open models in closed environments, organizations should be aware of several common pitfalls. One of the most significant challenges is ensuring the security and integrity of the models, as well as the data they process. This requires careful evaluation of the models' source code, dependencies, and potential vulnerabilities. Another challenge is managing the complexity of open models, which can be difficult to integrate with existing systems and workflows. Finally, organizations should be prepared to address potential intellectual property issues, as open models may be subject to various licensing agreements and restrictions.
