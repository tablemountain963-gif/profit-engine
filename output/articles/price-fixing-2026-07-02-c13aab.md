## Overview
Price fixing occurs when two or more competitors agree to set prices at a certain level, rather than competing with each other. This can lead to higher prices for consumers, reduced competition, and decreased innovation. In the case of the egg industry, the alleged price-fixing scheme involved three major producers, who reportedly worked together to limit supply and drive up prices. This resulted in significant profits for the producers, but higher costs for consumers and businesses that rely on eggs as a key ingredient.

## Why It Matters
The impact of price fixing can be far-reaching, affecting not just consumers but also businesses and the broader economy. When prices are artificially inflated, it can lead to reduced demand, decreased economic growth, and increased poverty. Furthermore, price fixing can also stifle innovation, as companies are less likely to invest in research and development when they can simply rely on collusion to maintain their market position. In the case of the egg industry, the alleged price-fixing scheme may have cost consumers hundreds of millions of dollars in excess prices, and potentially harmed businesses that rely on eggs as a key ingredient.
