## Overview
Cherry Profile keycaps are designed specifically for MX switch keyboards, offering a unique blend of ergonomics and aesthetics. With a focus on durability and tactile feedback, Cherry Profile keycaps have become a staple among mechanical keyboard enthusiasts. A key aspect of Cherry Profile keycaps is their compatibility with a wide range of MX switch keyboards, including those from popular manufacturers. 

## Why It Matters
The Cherry Profile is significant because it offers a distinct typing experience. With a total of 4 rows, the Cherry Profile provides a gradual incline from the bottom row to the top, allowing for comfortable typing and reduced finger fatigue. This design choice is in contrast to other keycap profiles, which may have a more abrupt change in height between rows. For example, some keycap profiles have as few as 3 rows, which can result in a less comfortable typing experience. 

In terms of durability, Cherry Profile keycaps are often made from high-quality materials, such as PBT (polycarbonate-butylene-terephthalate), which is more resistant to wear and tear than ABS (acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene). This results in keycaps that can withstand heavy use and maintain their texture and appearance over time. 

## How to Start
To get started with Cherry Profile keycaps, it's essential to consider the type of MX switch keyboard you have. Cherry Profile keycaps are designed specifically for MX switch keyboards, so it's crucial to ensure compatibility before making a purchase. Additionally, consider the material and design of the keycaps, as well as any additional features, such as backlighting or macro keys. 

When selecting a keycap set, consider the number of keys included. A standard 104-key set may not be sufficient for all users, especially those who require additional keys for specific applications or workflows. In such cases, a 128-key set may be more suitable. 

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall when purchasing Cherry Profile keycaps is assuming that all keycap sets are created equal. However, the quality and design of the keycaps can vary significantly between manufacturers. Additionally, some keycap sets may not be compatible with certain MX switch keyboards, which can result in a poor typing experience. 

Another common issue is the lack of consideration for ergonomics. Cherry Profile keycaps are designed to provide a comfortable typing experience, but this can be negated if the keycaps are not properly aligned or if the keyboard is not adjusted to the user's preferences.
