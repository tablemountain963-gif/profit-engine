87% of Git repositories use Conventional Commits, a specification that dictates a specific format for commit messages. However, a growing number of developers argue that this approach encourages focus on the wrong things, prioritizing consistency over meaningful communication. In fact, a recent discussion on Hacker News highlighted the limitations of Conventional Commits, with some developers suggesting that it leads to a culture of pointless commits and unnecessary complexity.

## Overview
Conventional Commits is a widely adopted specification that provides a set of rules for writing commit messages. The idea behind it is to create a standardized format that makes it easier to understand the purpose and content of each commit. The specification dictates that commit messages should follow a specific format, including a type (such as "fix" or "feat"), a brief description, and an optional body. While Conventional Commits has been widely adopted, some developers argue that it has become a mindless ritual, where the focus is on following the rules rather than communicating meaningful information.

## Why It Matters
The debate around Conventional Commits is not just about commit messages; it's about the culture and values of software development teams. When teams prioritize consistency over communication, they may end up with a repository that is easy to parse but difficult to understand. According to some critics, Conventional Commits encourages a culture of "commit message theater," where developers focus on writing perfect commit messages rather than writing good code. This can lead to a range of problems, including unnecessary complexity, pointless commits, and a lack of meaningful communication among team members.

## How to Start
If you're looking to move away from Conventional Commits, the first step is to re-evaluate your team's values and priorities. Ask yourself what matters most: consistency, communication, or something else entirely. Consider adopting a more flexible approach to commit messages, one that prioritizes clarity and meaning over strict adherence to a specification. You may also want to explore alternative approaches, such as scoped commits or semantic commits, which prioritize the content and purpose of each commit over its format.

## Common Pitfalls
One of the biggest pitfalls of Conventional Commits is the tendency to prioritize consistency over communication. This can lead to a range of problems, including:
* Pointless commits: When developers focus on writing perfect commit messages, they may end up creating unnecessary commits that don't add meaningful value to the repository.
* Unnecessary complexity: Conventional Commits can lead to a culture of over-engineering, where developers create complex solutions to simple problems in order to follow the rules.
* Lack of clarity: When teams prioritize consistency over communication, they may end up with commit messages that are easy to parse but difficult to understand.
