### 1,400 Years of Art and Faith: Unpacking the Grande Bible
The Grande Bible, a medieval manuscript from around 1395-1401, contains 256 intricate folios, each a testament to the art and craftsmanship of the time. One particular folio, numbered 256r, has garnered attention for its depiction of dragons, sparking interest in the intersection of medieval art and faith. This manuscript, housed in the Bibliothèque nationale de France, is a prime example of the Grande Bible historiale complétée, a comprehensive collection of biblical stories and interpretations.

## Overview
The Grande Bible historiale complétée is a medieval illuminated manuscript that showcases the artistic and theological endeavors of the late 14th century. Created in Paris between 1395 and 1401, this manuscript is a valuable resource for understanding the medieval perspective on biblical stories and their accompanying artwork. The inclusion of fantastical creatures, such as dragons, alongside traditional biblical narratives highlights the complex and often contradictory nature of medieval art and faith.
