## Overview
The Florida agricultural industry is a complex and multifaceted sector, with various crops, livestock, and farming practices. The state is a leading producer of citrus fruits, tomatoes, and strawberries, with a significant portion of its agricultural land dedicated to these crops. ## Common Pitfalls
One of the common pitfalls in the Florida agricultural industry is the lack of planning and preparedness for natural disasters, such as hurricanes and droughts. Farmers and agricultural businesses must be aware of the risks and take necessary precautions to mitigate their impact. Another pitfall is the over-reliance on a single crop or farming practice, which can make the industry vulnerable to market fluctuations and environmental changes. For example, the state's citrus industry has been severely impacted by citrus greening disease, highlighting the need for diversification and innovation in the industry.
