2.5 years passed between the proclamation of freedom on January 1, 1863, and the actual emancipation of over 250,000 enslaved people in Texas. This delay highlights the disparity between legal freedom and practical reality, a gap that still exists today in various forms.

## Overview
The concept of freedom is often viewed as an absolute state, but in reality, it exists on a spectrum. The history of emancipation in the United States, marked by events like Juneteenth, shows that freedom was not uniformly applied, with many individuals and groups facing continued oppression despite legal declarations. This nuanced understanding of freedom is crucial for addressing its complexities.

## Why It Matters
Freedom matters because it directly impacts the well-being, opportunities, and dignity of individuals and communities. The delay in emancipation for 250,000 people in Texas resulted in continued suffering and inequality. Similarly, contemporary issues like systemic racism, economic inequality, and social injustice all relate to the concept of freedom and its uneven distribution. Understanding the history and ongoing struggles for freedom can inform strategies for creating a more equitable society.

## How to Start
Embracing the journey towards greater freedom involves several steps. Firstly, educating oneself about the historical and current struggles for freedom is essential. This includes learning about the experiences of marginalized communities and the ways in which systemic barriers limit their freedom. Secondly, engaging in open and respectful dialogue with others about these issues can help build empathy and a collective understanding of the importance of freedom. Finally, supporting organizations and initiatives that work towards dismantling systemic barriers to freedom can be a tangible way to contribute to the cause.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall in the pursuit of freedom is the assumption that legal equality automatically translates to practical freedom. The example of the 2.5-year delay in Texas illustrates this disconnect. Another pitfall is overlooking the intersections of different forms of oppression, such as racism, sexism, and economic inequality, which can compound to further limit an individual's or group's freedom. Lastly, complacency and the belief that freedom is someone else's responsibility can hinder progress.
