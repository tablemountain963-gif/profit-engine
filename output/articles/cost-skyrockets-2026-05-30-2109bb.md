Corporate America is spending an average of $1.2 million per year on AI solutions, with some companies allocating as much as 20% of their IT budget to AI initiatives. However, the cost of implementing and maintaining AI systems is skyrocketing, with some reports indicating a 300% increase in costs over the past two years alone. This has led to a significant shift in how companies approach AI adoption, with many now rationing AI as a cost-saving measure.

## Overview
The rising cost of AI is attributed to several factors, including the increasing complexity of AI models, the need for specialized hardware and software, and the high demand for skilled AI talent. As a result, companies are being forced to re-evaluate their AI strategies and prioritize their investments. This has led to a trend of AI rationing, where companies are limiting their use of AI to only the most critical applications.

According to recent reports, the cost of training a single AI model can range from $100,000 to over $1 million, depending on the complexity of the model and the amount of data required. Additionally, the cost of maintaining and updating AI systems can add up quickly, with some companies reporting annual maintenance costs of over $500,000. These costs are leading many companies to take a more cautious approach to AI adoption, with some opting to outsource their AI needs to third-party providers.

## Why It Matters
The rising cost of AI has significant implications for businesses, as it can impact their ability to compete in the market. Companies that are unable to afford the high costs of AI may find themselves at a disadvantage compared to their competitors who have already adopted AI solutions. Furthermore, the rationing of AI can also limit the potential benefits of AI, such as improved efficiency and decision-making.

A study by a leading research firm found that companies that have adopted AI have seen an average increase of 15% in productivity and 10% in revenue. However, these benefits come at a cost, and companies must carefully weigh the trade-offs between investing in AI and other business priorities. As the cost of AI continues to rise, companies will need to develop strategies to manage these costs and ensure that their AI investments are generating a strong return.

## How to Start
For companies looking to adopt AI despite the rising costs, there are several strategies that can help. One approach is to start small, by identifying a specific business problem that can be addressed through AI and piloting a solution. This can help to build a business case for further investment in AI and demonstrate the potential benefits of AI to stakeholders.

Another approach is to leverage cloud-based AI services, which can provide access to AI capabilities without the need for significant upfront investment. These services can also provide scalability and flexibility, allowing companies to quickly adapt to changing business needs. Additionally, companies can also consider partnering with AI startups or research institutions to access new AI technologies and expertise.

## Common Pitfalls
Despite the potential benefits of AI, there are several common pitfalls that companies should avoid. One of the most significant pitfalls is the assumption that AI will automatically generate business value. In reality, AI requires significant investment in data quality, integration, and governance to generate meaningful insights.

Another pitfall is the failure to develop a clear AI strategy, which can lead to fragmented and ineffective AI initiatives. Companies should also be aware of the potential risks associated with AI, such as bias and job displacement, and develop strategies to mitigate these risks. By being aware of these pitfalls, companies can avoid common mistakes and ensure that their AI investments generate a strong return.

## Recommendations
To manage the rising cost of AI, companies can consider investing in several key product categories, including:
* **Cloud-based AI platforms**, which provide scalable and flexible access to AI capabilities
* **Data integration tools**, which help to ensure high-quality data and reduce the costs associated with data preparation
* **AI governance software**, which provides visibility and control over AI initiatives and helps to mitigate risks
* **Machine learning frameworks**, which provide pre-built models and algorithms to accelerate AI development
* **Specialized AI hardware**, which can provide significant performance improvements for certain AI workloads

By investing in these product categories, companies can help to manage the rising cost of AI and ensure that their AI investments generate a strong return. The next step is to conduct a thorough review of your company's AI strategy and identify areas where these product categories can be leveraged to drive business value.
