## Overview
Residential proxy SDKs are software development kits that allow apps to route user traffic through residential IP addresses, making it appear as if the traffic is coming from a home network rather than a data center. This can be used for various purposes, including data collection, advertising, and content restriction bypassing. However, it also poses significant risks to user privacy and security.

## Why It Matters
The presence of residential proxy SDKs in smart TV apps is a concern because it can lead to unauthorized data collection, IP address spoofing, and even malware distribution. Users are often unaware of these SDKs and the potential risks they pose, as they are embedded deep within the app's code. With 2,058 smart TV apps selling user IP addresses, the potential for exploitation is vast. Furthermore, the use of residential proxy SDKs can also facilitate malicious activities such as credential stuffing, account takeovers, and phishing attacks.
