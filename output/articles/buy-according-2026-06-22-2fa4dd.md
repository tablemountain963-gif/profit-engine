## How to Start
If you're interested in investing in AI software stocks, it's essential to start by doing your research. Look into companies that are leaders in the AI software space, such as those that specialize in machine learning, natural language processing, or computer vision. You should also consider the company's financials, management team, and competitive landscape. For example, you can use the GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price) approach to identify stocks that offer a balance of growth and value. Additionally, you can use technical analysis tools, such as the Relative Strength Index (RSI), to identify trends and patterns in the market.

## Common Pitfalls
One of the common pitfalls of investing in AI software stocks is the risk of overhyping. With so much excitement surrounding AI technology, it's easy to get caught up in the hype and invest in companies that may not have a solid foundation. It's essential to separate the signal from the noise and focus on companies with a proven track record and a clear path to growth. Another pitfall is the lack of diversification, as investing too heavily in one stock or sector can increase risk. To mitigate this, you can use a diversification strategy, such as the core-satellite approach, to spread your investments across different asset classes and sectors.
