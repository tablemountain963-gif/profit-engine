## Overview
The Fifth Amendment is a fundamental component of the US Constitution, providing crucial protections for individuals against government overreach. It guarantees the right to a fair trial, protection against self-incrimination, and due process in cases of detention or deportation. The amendment also prohibits the government from taking private property without just compensation. In the context of immigration, the Fifth Amendment plays a critical role in ensuring that individuals are treated fairly and humanely, regardless of their nationality or immigration status.
