## How to Start
So, how can you start using more inclusive language in your daily interactions? It begins with education and a willingness to learn. Start by familiarizing yourself with the different types of pronouns, such as he/him, she/her, they/them, and ze/hir. Practice using these pronouns in low-stakes situations, such as in online forums or with friends who are open to feedback. You can also take online courses or attend workshops that focus on inclusive language and communication.

## Common Pitfalls
One of the most common pitfalls when it comes to using inclusive language is making assumptions about someone's pronouns based on their appearance or name. This can lead to misgendering and hurt feelings. Another pitfall is using language that is overly formal or awkward, such as using "they" as a singular pronoun in a way that sounds unnatural. To avoid these pitfalls, it's essential to listen to others, ask for feedback, and be open to correction.
