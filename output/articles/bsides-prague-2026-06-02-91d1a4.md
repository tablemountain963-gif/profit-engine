Every year, over 1,200 security experts and enthusiasts gather at BSides Prague, a premier cybersecurity conference, to discuss the latest trends and challenges in the field. This year, one of the most talked-about topics was the impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) on cybersecurity, with a specific focus on the "Torment Nexus" – a hypothetical AI system that could potentially wreak havoc on the world. 

## Overview
BSides Prague is part of a global series of community-driven cybersecurity conferences that aim to provide a platform for experts and enthusiasts to share knowledge, ideas, and experiences. The conference features a range of talks, workshops, and discussions on various topics related to cybersecurity, including AI, threat intelligence, and incident response. This year's conference saw a significant focus on AI, with many speakers discussing its potential applications and risks in the context of cybersecurity.

## Why It Matters
The discussion around AI's impact on cybersecurity is crucial, as the technology has the potential to both improve and compromise security systems. On one hand, AI can be used to enhance threat detection and incident response, helping to protect against increasingly sophisticated cyber attacks. On the other hand, AI can also be used to launch more complex and targeted attacks, making it easier for hackers to breach security systems. The concept of the "Torment Nexus" highlights the potential risks of AI in cybersecurity, emphasizing the need for careful consideration and planning when developing and implementing AI systems.

## How to Start
For those interested in learning more about AI's impact on cybersecurity, a good starting point would be to explore online resources and courses that provide an introduction to the topic. This could include watching talks and presentations from conferences like BSides Prague, as well as taking online courses that cover the basics of AI and cybersecurity. Additionally, joining online communities and forums related to cybersecurity and AI can provide valuable opportunities to connect with experts and enthusiasts, and stay up-to-date with the latest developments in the field.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall in the development and implementation of AI systems in cybersecurity is the failure to consider the potential risks and consequences. This can include overlooking the potential for AI to be used in malicious ways, or underestimating the complexity and sophistication of AI-powered attacks. Another pitfall is the lack of transparency and accountability in AI decision-making processes, which can make it difficult to understand and mitigate potential risks. To avoid these pitfalls, it's essential to approach AI development and implementation in a careful and considered manner, prioritizing transparency, accountability, and ongoing evaluation and testing.
