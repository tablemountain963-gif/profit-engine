7% passive income sounds like a dream, especially when compared to the 1% yield offered by popular index funds like VOO. But what if you could tap into this higher yield without sacrificing the benefits of a diversified investment portfolio?

## Overview
VOO, a popular index fund, has been a staple in many investors' portfolios due to its broad diversification and low fees. However, its relatively low yield has led some investors to explore alternative options. Two BlackRock ETFs, in particular, have caught the attention of income-seeking investors, offering yields of over 7%. This significant difference in yield can result in substantially higher returns over time, especially for those who rely on their investments for regular income.

## Why It Matters
For investors seeking regular income from their investments, the difference between a 1% and 7% yield can be substantial. Consider an investment of $100,000: with a 1% yield, you would earn $1,000 in a year, whereas a 7% yield would generate $7,000. This extra $6,000 can make a significant difference for those living off their investments or seeking to grow their wealth more rapidly.

## How to Start
Investing in ETFs with higher yields involves understanding the underlying assets and the associated risks. These ETFs often focus on specific sectors or asset classes that are known for their higher income potential, such as real estate investment trusts (REITs), dividend-paying stocks, or corporate bonds. Before investing, it's crucial to assess your personal risk tolerance and financial goals to ensure that these investments align with your overall strategy.

## Common Pitfalls
While the prospect of higher yields is attractive, it's essential to be aware of the potential pitfalls. Higher-yielding investments often come with higher risk, such as the possibility of default in the case of bond ETFs or the volatility associated with stock ETFs. Additionally, some ETFs may have higher fees than their lower-yielding counterparts, which can eat into your returns over time. It's also important to consider the tax implications of your investments, as higher-yielding investments may result in a larger tax burden.

## Recommendations
For those looking to unlock higher yields without sacrificing diversification, several product categories are worth considering:
- **High-Yield Bond ETFs**: These offer exposure to a portfolio of high-yield bonds, providing regular income with a higher yield than traditional bonds.
- **Dividend-Focused Equity ETFs**: By investing in stocks with a history of paying consistent dividends, these ETFs can offer a relatively stable source of income.
- **Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) ETFs**: REITs are known for their income-generating potential, and ETFs that track this sector can provide a diversified portfolio of properties without the need for direct property investment.
- **Preferred Stock ETFs**: These often offer higher yields than common stocks and can provide a regular income stream with relatively lower volatility.
- **Closed-End Fund (CEF) ETFs**: CEFs can invest in a wide range of assets and often use leverage to enhance yields, making them an option for those seeking higher income.

To get started, consider your investment objectives, risk tolerance, and the current market conditions. Then, explore the various ETF options within these categories, ensuring they align with your financial goals. With careful planning and a clear understanding of the potential risks and rewards, you can unlock the potential of higher-yielding investments and move closer to achieving your financial objectives. Your next step should be to consult with a financial advisor or conduct thorough research on these product categories to make informed investment decisions.
