In the spring of 2026, a staggering 40% of students in multiple UC Berkeley computer science classes received failing grades, marking a significant departure from the department's traditional grading guidelines. This sudden surge in failing grades has sparked a heated debate about the impact of AI on math skills and the overall learning experience in CS classes.

## Overview
The University of California, Berkeley, is renowned for its rigorous computer science program, attracting top talent from around the world. However, the increasing reliance on AI tools in CS classes has led to a disturbing trend: dwindling math skills and soaring failing grades. According to recent data, the percentage of failing grades in multiple CS classes has increased significantly compared to past semesters. This raises important questions about the role of AI in education and its potential consequences on student learning outcomes.

## Why It Matters
The decline in math skills among CS students is particularly alarming, as it can have long-term consequences for their future careers. Mathematics is a fundamental building block of computer science, and a strong foundation in math is essential for success in the field. The over-reliance on AI tools can create a false sense of security, leading students to neglect their math skills and ultimately struggle with more advanced concepts. Furthermore, the high failing grades can also have a negative impact on student morale and motivation, potentially leading to a decrease in student retention and graduation rates.

## How to Start
To address this issue, educators and students must take a step back and reassess their approach to learning and teaching CS. This can involve setting clear expectations about the use of AI tools, providing additional support for students who struggle with math, and incorporating more hands-on, math-intensive exercises into the curriculum. By striking a balance between the use of AI tools and traditional math skills, students can develop a more well-rounded understanding of CS concepts and improve their overall learning outcomes.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall in CS education is the over-reliance on AI-powered tools, such as automatic coding assistants and math solvers. While these tools can be incredibly helpful in certain situations, they can also create a crutch that prevents students from developing their own math skills. Another pitfall is the lack of transparency about the use of AI tools, which can lead to a lack of accountability and a false sense of accomplishment among students. To avoid these pitfalls, educators must be aware of the potential risks and take steps to mitigate them.

## Recommendations
To improve math skills and reduce the reliance on AI tools, students can benefit from using a range of educational resources, including:
* **Online math tutorials**, which can provide personalized support and feedback
* **Programming textbooks**, which can offer a comprehensive introduction to CS concepts
* **Math practice software**, which can help students develop their math skills through interactive exercises
* **Coding bootcamp courses**, which can provide hands-on experience with CS concepts
* **Educational laptops**, which can provide students with a dedicated device for learning and practicing CS skills

By leveraging these resources and taking a more balanced approach to CS education, students can develop the math skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the field. The next step is to start exploring these resources and to begin developing a more well-rounded understanding of CS concepts. By taking this proactive approach, students can set themselves up for success and mitigate the unintended consequences of AI in education.
