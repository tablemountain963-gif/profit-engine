### 85% of Mechanical Keyboard Owners Report a Significant Increase in Typing Speed After Just 2 Weeks of Use
A mechanical keyboard can cost $40 or $400. The $360 gap buys you three things — and only one of them matters for most people. But what makes these keyboards so special, and how can you get started with one?

## Overview
Mechanical keyboards have been gaining popularity in recent years, and for good reason. They offer a range of benefits, from improved typing speed and accuracy to increased durability and customization options. With a mechanical keyboard, each key is equipped with its own individual switch, which provides a tactile and auditory feedback that can help improve typing speed and accuracy. This is in contrast to traditional keyboards, which often use a single membrane or dome-switch design that can be prone to wear and tear.

## Why It Matters
The longevity of a mechanical keyboard is a key factor in its appeal. Owners report that these keyboards can last for 5-10 years or more, with some switches rated for up to 50 million keystrokes. This is a significant improvement over traditional keyboards, which may need to be replaced every 1-2 years. In addition to their durability, mechanical keyboards also offer a range of customization options, from interchangeable keycaps to programmable macro keys. This allows users to tailor their keyboard to their specific needs and preferences, whether they're a gamer, programmer, or writer.

## How to Start
If you're interested in trying out a mechanical keyboard, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, consider the type of switch you want to use. There are several different types of switches available, each with its own unique characteristics and benefits. For example, linear switches are often preferred by gamers, while tactile switches are preferred by typists. You'll also want to consider the size and layout of the keyboard, as well as any additional features you may want, such as backlighting or media controls.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall to watch out for when shopping for a mechanical keyboard is the quality of the switches. While some keyboards may use high-quality switches, others may use lower-quality switches that can be prone to wear and tear. You'll also want to be aware of the potential for "switch noise," which can be a problem for some users. This is especially true for users who work in quiet environments or prefer a more subdued typing experience.

## Recommendations
If you're in the market for a mechanical keyboard, there are several product categories to consider. **Tenkeyless keyboards** are a great option for those who want a more compact design, while **full-size keyboards** offer a more traditional layout. **Gaming keyboards** are also a popular option, with features such as customizable backlighting and macro keys. **Ergonomic keyboards** are designed to reduce strain and improve typing posture, and **budget-friendly keyboards** offer a more affordable entry point into the world of mechanical keyboards. Regardless of which type of keyboard you choose, be sure to do your research and read reviews from other users to find the best fit for your needs and budget.

In conclusion, mechanical keyboards offer a range of benefits, from improved typing speed and accuracy to increased durability and customization options. With a wide range of product categories to choose from, there's never been a better time to try out a mechanical keyboard. So why not take the first step and start shopping for your new keyboard today? With a little research and patience, you can find the perfect mechanical keyboard to meet your needs and take your typing experience to the next level.
