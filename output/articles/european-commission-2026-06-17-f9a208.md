## Overview
The European Commission's move towards embracing closed-source, commercial social media platforms has raised eyebrows among advocates of open-source technologies and digital sovereignty. This shift could have far-reaching implications for the EU's digital landscape, potentially undermining the region's efforts to promote technological independence and self-determination. With the EU's digital market valued at over 450 billion euros, the stakes are high, and the Commission's decisions will likely have significant consequences for the region's economy and citizens.

## Why It Matters
The European Commission's approach to social media regulation could have significant trade-offs. On one hand, mandatory age verification may help reduce online harassment and protect minors, with some studies suggesting a 20-30% reduction in online abuse. On the other hand, this approach may also lead to increased costs for social media platforms, potentially resulting in higher fees for users or reduced services. Furthermore, the reliance on closed-source, commercial solutions may undermine the EU's technology sovereignty goals, potentially creating dependencies on foreign technologies and undermining the region's digital autonomy.

To avoid these pitfalls, the Commission should prioritize open-source technologies, ensure transparency in its decision-making process, and engage with stakeholders to ensure that the EU's digital direction aligns with its sovereignty goals.
