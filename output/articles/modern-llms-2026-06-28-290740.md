A staggering 90% of the data used to train Modern LLMs (Large Language Models) is based on information that is at least two years old. This means that when you interact with these models, they are making predictions and generating text based on a snapshot of the world that is locked in time. Despite this limitation, Modern LLMs can still uncover surprising connections and insights, making them a powerful tool for anyone looking to analyze and understand complex data.

## Overview
Modern LLMs work by interpolating based on the training data they have been given. This means that they are able to identify patterns and relationships within the data, even if they are not explicitly stated. For example, a Modern LLM might be trained on a dataset of text from the internet, and then use that training to generate new text that is similar in style and structure. This can be a powerful tool for tasks such as language translation, text summarization, and even creative writing.

## Why It Matters
The ability of Modern LLMs to uncover hidden connections and insights makes them a valuable tool for a wide range of applications. For instance, they can be used to analyze large datasets and identify patterns that might not be immediately apparent to human researchers. This can be particularly useful in fields such as medicine, finance, and social science, where complex datasets are common. Additionally, Modern LLMs can be used to generate text that is similar in style and structure to a given dataset, making them a useful tool for tasks such as content generation and language translation.

## How to Start
Getting started with Modern LLMs can seem daunting, but it doesn't have to be. One approach is to start by exploring the different types of models that are available, and identifying which one is best suited to your specific needs. For example, some models are designed specifically for language translation, while others are better suited to tasks such as text summarization or content generation. It's also important to consider the size and complexity of the dataset that you will be working with, as well as the computational resources that you have available.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall when working with Modern LLMs is the assumption that they are able to understand the context and nuances of human language. While these models are incredibly powerful, they are still limited by the data that they have been trained on, and can sometimes struggle to understand the subtleties of human communication. Another pitfall is the failure to consider the potential biases and limitations of the training data, which can result in models that are biased or inaccurate.
