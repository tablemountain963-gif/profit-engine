A staggering 73% of code generated by AI tools requires human review to ensure accuracy and reliability. This statistic highlights the limitations of relying solely on AI for software development, and underscores the importance of viable arguments in evaluating the effectiveness of AI-generated code. 

## Overview
The use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in software development has gained significant traction in recent years, with many proponents arguing that it can improve efficiency and reduce costs. However, skeptics argue that reviewing AI-generated code is not a viable argument for several reasons. One key concern is that AI-generated code often requires significant human review and editing to ensure that it meets the required standards. This process can be time-consuming and may negate the potential benefits of using AI in the first place.

## Why It Matters
The viability of AI-generated code is a critical issue in software development, as it has significant implications for the quality and reliability of the final product. If AI-generated code is not thoroughly reviewed and tested, it can lead to errors, bugs, and security vulnerabilities that can have serious consequences. Furthermore, the use of AI-generated code can also raise intellectual property (IP) concerns, as the ownership and copyright of the code may be unclear. According to some experts, these IP laws problems are highly problematic and can have significant legal and financial implications.

## How to Start
To develop a viable argument for or against the use of AI-generated code, it is essential to understand the current state of AI technology and its limitations. This involves reviewing the existing literature and research on AI-generated code, as well as examining the experiences of other developers who have used AI tools in their projects. It is also crucial to consider the specific requirements of the project, including the level of complexity, the need for customization, and the potential risks and liabilities.

## Common Pitfalls
One common pitfall in evaluating the viability of AI-generated code is to overestimate the capabilities of AI tools. While AI has made significant progress in recent years, it is still not a replacement for human judgment and expertise. Another common mistake is to underestimate the time and effort required to review and edit AI-generated code. This can lead to delays and cost overruns, which can have significant consequences for the project.
