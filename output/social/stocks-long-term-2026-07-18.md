Most long-term investors rely on a handful of stocks like Apple, Microsoft, and Johnson & Johnson. But is that enough diversification? The 60/30/10 rule can provide a more balanced approach, allocating 60% to S&P 500 index funds, 30% to mid caps like Visa, and 10% to small caps like Shopify. Dollar-cost averaging can reduce timing risk, but implementing it monthly can decrease returns by 5% annually. Historically, the S&P 500 has outperformed 82% of actively managed funds over 10-year periods, with an average annual return of 10.5%. Tax efficiency is also crucial, with tax-deferred accounts saving 12% in taxes on tax-inefficient assets like REITs.
